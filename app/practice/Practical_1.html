Lesson 1. Training the population in the field of protection from natural and man-made emergencies

1. Rights and responsibilities of citizens in the field of protection from emergency situations

In the event of an emergency, citizens of the Republic of Belarus have the right to protection of life and property; compensation and benefits; free medical care and free state social insurance; pension provision, etc.

In emergency situations, citizens of Belarus are required to know the methods, means of protection and techniques for providing first aid; comply with legislation in the field of protection from emergency situations; follow the established rules of conduct in the event of a threat or occurrence of emergency situations, etc.

Of great importance in the implementation of the rights and obligations of citizens are government bodies, in particular republican government bodies and other government organizations subordinate to the Council of Ministers of the Republic of Belarus.

Responsibilities of the republican government bodies and other state organizations subordinate to the Council of Ministers of the Republic of Belarus in the field of protecting the population and territories from emergency situations:

a) in relation to subordinate organizations:

  • develop and implement organizational and engineering measures to improve the sustainability of the industry in emergency situations;
  • approve and issue, in accordance with the requirements of the legislation of the Republic of Belarus, legal acts on the safety of production, technological processes, products, as well as the protection of employees of organizations from emergency situations;
  • ensure the development and implementation of measures to strengthen radiation, chemical, medical and biological, explosive, fire, and environmental safety;
  • finance and provide measures to prevent emergency situations and conduct emergency rescue and other urgent work in emergency situations;
  • organize and ensure the implementation of research, design, experimental design and testing work;

b) in relation to other organizations that are part of the industry:

  • provide methodological guidance in resolving issues related to protecting employees of organizations from emergency situations, increasing the sustainability and safety of the functioning of organizations;
  • develop and communicate to organizations industry requirements and legal acts on issues of preventing and eliminating emergency situations, and protecting employees of organizations from these situations.

2. Classification of natural emergencies, their sources, causes, brief description

In the Republic of Belarus, emergency situations are classified according to two main criteria:

1. Depending on the territorial distribution (local, regional, national and cross-border), the volume of material damage (from over forty basic units to over 0.5 million basic units), the number of affected people (from 10 to over 500 people)

2. By nature of origin (man-made, natural).

Man-made emergencies – transport accidents (disasters), fires, unprovoked explosions or their threat, accidents with the release (threat of release) of hazardous chemical, radioactive, biological substances, sudden destruction of structures and buildings, accidents on utility networks and life support structures, hydrodynamic accidents on dams, embankments and other engineering structures.

Natural emergencies are dangerous geological, meteorological, hydrological phenomena, degradation of soils or subsoil, natural fires, changes in the state of the air basin, infectious diseases of people and farm animals, mass damage to agricultural plants and forests by diseases or pests, changes in the state of water resources and the biosphere.

The causes of natural emergencies are characterized by natural and anthropogenic hazards.

Natural hazards include endogenous and exogenous processes.

  • Endogenous processes raise individual sections of the earth's crust. The main source of energy for endogenous processes is the internal heat in the bowels of the Earth. These processes cause volcanic activity, earthquakes, etc.
  • Exogenous processes destroy the earth's crust. Simultaneously with the destruction, there is a process of accumulation of destruction products. Exogenous natural disasters caused by solar energy and gravity (floods, storms, landslides, droughts, etc.)

Anthropogenic hazards. Thus, according to international statistics, about 80% of landslides are associated with human activity. As a result of deforestation, mudflow activity increases, and flood volumes increase. At present, the scale of use of natural resources has increased significantly, as a result, features of a global environmental crisis have become noticeably evident.

Natural emergencies in the Republic of Belarus. The most probable natural disasters in Belarus are: hydrological (floods), meteorological (strong winds, storms, hurricanes, heavy rainfall, hail, heavy snowfall, drought, frost), fires (forest fires, peat fires), geological (non-destructive and few earthquakes).

Infectious diseases occupy a special place among natural emergencies.

The danger of infectious diseases of people and animals (epidemics and epizootics). There are more than 500 natural foci of anthrax in the republic, there are natural foci of rabies, which can lead to mass diseases of domestic animals and people.

Damage to agricultural plants by diseases and pests (epiphytoties). Mass diseases of agricultural crops by brown rust and late blight are most common in the Brest, Grodno, and Gomel regions. The emergence of epizootic and epiphytotic foci on the territory of the republic may lead to a reduction in agricultural production by 5-10%.

3. Recommendations for citizens' actions in the event of a threat or occurrence of natural emergencies

Flood. When notified. A flood warning can be given by the signal "Attention everyone! " transmitted by ­sirens, intermittent horns of enterprises and vehicles. Having heard the signal, turn on the radio, TV and listen to the information and instructions for the population.

Upon receiving information about the start of the evacuation, you should quickly get ready and take with you: a bag with documents and money; a first aid ­kit; a three-day supply of food; bed linen and toiletries; a set of outerwear and shoes.

All evacuees must arrive at the evacuation point by the appointed time ­for registration and dispatch to a safe area. Depending on the current situation, the population is evacuated by specially designated transport or on foot.

Upon arrival at the final destination, registration takes place and transportation to temporary accommodation is organized ­.

In case of sudden flooding, it is recommended to occupy the nearest safe elevated place as quickly as possible ­and be prepared for an organized evacuation by water using various floating craft or on foot along fords. In such a situation, one should not give in to panic or lose self-control.

It is necessary to take measures to enable rescuers to promptly detect people cut off by water and in need of help. During daylight hours, this is achieved ­by hanging a white or colored cloth in a high place, and at night - by giving light signals.

Until help arrives, people in the flood zone should stay on the upper floors and roofs of buildings, trees and other elevated places. Usually, staying ­in the flood zone lasts until the water recedes or help arrives.

When in water Don't panic. Hold on to floating objects, try to tie them into a raft and climb onto it, push away dangerous objects with sharp protruding parts, try to swim to a safe place.

Hurricanes, storms, tornadoes. The main signs of the occurrence of hurricanes, storms and tornadoes are: an increase in wind speed and a sharp drop in atmospheric pressure; torrential rains and storm surges; violent snowfall and ground dust.

When warning. Information about the threat of hurricanes, storms and tornadoes carries information about the ­onset of a natural phenomenon in a specific area, the possible nature of its action and the behavior of people in the developing situation.

On the windward side of buildings, windows, doors, attic hatches and ventilation openings are tightly closed ­. Window and display glass is pasted over, protected with shutters or shields. To equalize the internal pressure, doors and windows on the leeward side of buildings are opened.

It is necessary to prepare electric ­lanterns, candles, camping stoves, food supplies, drinking water and medicines.

Upon receiving information about the immediate approach of a hurricane or severe storm, people take previously prepared places in buildings or shelters, and in In case of a tornado - only basements and underground structures.

In case of a sudden hurricane. When in a building, beware of injuries from shards of window glass. In case of strong gusts of wind, it is necessary to move away from windows and take a place in wall niches, doorways ­or stand close to the wall, and also use durable furniture and mattresses.

If you are forced to stay outdoors ­, it is advisable to move as far away from buildings as possible and take shelter in ravines, pits, ditches, canals, roadside ditches; in this case, you need to lie down in them and press yourself tightly to the ground. Such actions significantly reduce the number of injuries caused by the projectile action of hurricanes and storms, and also fully provide protection from flying shards of glass, bricks and other objects. It is not recommended to be on bridges, in places in close proximity to objects containing chemically hazardous and flammable substances. You can protect yourself from flying debris and shards of glass with plywood sheets, cardboard and plastic boxes, boards and other improvised means (Fig. 6).  

When hurricanes and storms are accompanied by thunderstorms, situations should be avoided in which ­the probability of electric shock increases. Therefore, one should not take shelter under isolated trees, poles and masts, or approach power transmission line supports too closely.
