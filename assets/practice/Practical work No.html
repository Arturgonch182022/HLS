Practical work 1

Defeat by poisonous substances. Ways of penetration into the body, signs, first aid, care. Emergency care in acute poisoning

Purpose of the work: to familiarise the trainees with the actions of nerve and general poisonous substances, as well as in training in pre-hospital care for poisoning by both military and household poisons. During the study of this topic, special attention should be paid to the possibility of contact with some of these substances in everyday life. Students should be taught to recognise the signs of poisoning and to administer first aid. The prevention of poisoning should also be one of the key points.

Theory of work

1. First aid in case of poisoning by poisonous substances

Regardless of the type of poisonous substance (SP) used, the victim should immediately put on a serviceable gas mask. This will allow to stop further entry of OM into the body through respiratory organs, as well as protect the eyes, facial skin and partially scalp from the effects of harmful substances.

If the victim is in the immediate area of OM application, where the smallest drops of the substance could get on the face, it is first necessary to treat the skin of the face with liquid from the individual anti-chemical package (IPP-8) (Fig. 1), and only after that to put on a gas mask. Further actions on first aid are determined by the type of applied chemical agent and, as a rule, provide for the use of means from the individual first aid kit (Fig. 2). This may include administration of antidotes, eye wash, special skin treatment and other necessary measures depending on the nature of the lesion.

Timely application of personal protective equipment and effective first-priority actions are of key importance for saving life and health of victims in case of casualties in case of casualty exposure.

2. First aid procedure in case of respiratory organ damage

Asphyxiating chemical agents:

1. Affects through respiratory organs, causing sweet taste in the mouth, cough, dizziness, general weakness.

2. Develops pulmonary oedema in the ‘latent period’, after which breathing may deteriorate sharply, cough with sputum, headache, dyspnoea.

First aid: limit mobility, evacuate until the end of the latent period, provide warmth and rest. Artificial respiration is forbidden.

Poisonous substances of general poisonous action:

1. Affects only by inhalation, not through the skin.

2. Causes metallic taste, throat irritation, dizziness, weakness, nausea, convulsions, paralysis.

First aid: immediately put on a gas mask, give inhaled antidote, in case of respiratory arrest - artificial respiration, evacuation.

It is important to get the victims out of the contaminated area as soon as possible and provide them with qualified medical assistance. Correct first aid actions can save lives.

3. First aid procedure for injuries of the central nervous system

First aid:

1. In case of respiratory distress - perform artificial respiration.

2. In case of cardiac arrest - start cardiopulmonary resuscitation.

3. If seizures occur, try to prevent injury to the victim, but do not fix the limbs.

4. For nausea and vomiting, turn the victim's head to the side to prevent aspiration of vomit.

4. First aid procedures for skin lesions

First aid treatment:

1. For wounds, stop bleeding by applying a pressure dressing.

2. In case of burns - cool the affected area with cold running water for 10-20 minutes. 3.

3. In case of chemical burns - rinse the affected area with plenty of water.

4. In case of frostbite - carefully wrap the affected area, do not rub it.
