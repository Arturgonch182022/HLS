Class 1. Training of the population in the field of defence against natural and man-made emergencies

1. Rights and duties of citizens in the field of protection from emergency situations.

In the event of an emergency, citizens of the Republic of Belarus have the right to protection of life and property; compensation and benefits; free medical care and free State social insurance; pension security, etc.

In emergencies, citizens of Belarus are obliged to know the ways, means of protection and first aid techniques; to observe legislation on protection against emergencies; to comply with the established rules of behaviour in the event of the threat and occurrence of emergencies; and so on.

State authorities, in particular republican bodies of state administration and other state organisations subordinate to the Council of Ministers of the Republic of Belarus, play an important role in the implementation of the rights and obligations of citizens.

Responsibilities of the republican bodies of state administration and other state organisations subordinate to the Council of Ministers of the Republic of Belarus in the field of protection of the population and territories from emergency situations:

(a) In relation to subordinate organisations:

- develop and implement organisational, engineering and technical measures to increase the stability of the industry's functioning in emergency situations;

- approve and issue, in accordance with the requirements of the legislation of the Republic of Belarus, legal acts on safety of production, technological processes, products, as well as protection of employees of organisations from emergency situations;

- ensure the development and implementation of measures to strengthen radiation, chemical, medical and biological, explosion, fire and environmental safety;

- finance and ensure measures to prevent emergencies and carry out rescue and other urgent work in emergencies;

- organise and ensure research, design, development and testing activities;

b) in relation to other organisations within the industry:

- provide methodological guidance in solving the issues of protection of the organisations‘ employees from emergency situations, increasing the stability and safety of the organisations’ functioning;

- develop and bring to the attention of organisations sectoral requirements, legal acts on the issues of prevention and liquidation of emergency situations, protection of employees of organisations from these situations.

2. Classification of natural emergencies, their sources, causes of occurrence, brief characterisation

In the Republic of Belarus, emergencies are classified according to two main criteria:

1. Depending on the territorial spread (local, local, regional, republican and transboundary), the amount of material damage (from over forty basic units to over 0.5 million basic units), the number of affected people (from 10 to over 500 people).

2. By nature of origin (man-made, natural).

Man-made emergencies - transport accidents (disasters), fires, unprovoked explosions or their threat, accidents with the release (threat of release) of dangerous chemical, radioactive, biological substances, sudden destruction of structures and buildings, accidents on engineering networks and life support structures, hydrodynamic accidents on dams, dikes and other engineering structures.

Natural emergencies - dangerous geological, meteorological, hydrological phenomena, degradation of soil or subsoil, natural fires, changes in the state of the air basin, infectious diseases of people, farm animals, mass lesions of agricultural plants and forests by diseases or pests, changes in the state of water resources and the biosphere.

Classification of natural emergencies provides division of emergencies into classes, groups and types.

Causes of natural emergencies are characterised by natural and anthropogenic hazards.

Endogenous and exogenous processes can be attributed to natural hazards.

Endogenous processes uplift certain parts of the Earth's crust. The main source of energy of endogenous processes is internal heat in the Earth's interior. These processes cause volcanic activity, earthquakes, etc.

Exogenous processes destroy the Earth's crust. Simultaneously with destruction there is a process of accumulation of destruction products. Exogenous disasters are caused by solar energy and gravity (floods, storms, landslides, droughts, etc.).

Anthropogenic hazards. Thus, according to international statistics, about 80% of landslides are related to human activity. As a result of deforestation, the activity of mudflows increases, and the flood volume increases. At present, the scale of natural resources utilisation has significantly increased, and as a result, the features of the global ecological crisis have become tangible.

Natural emergencies in the Republic of Belarus. The most probable natural disasters in Belarus are: hydrological (floods), meteorological (strong winds, storms, hurricanes, heavy rainfall, hail, heavy snowfall, drought, frost), fires (forest fires, peat fires), geological (non-destructive and few earthquakes).

A special place among natural emergencies is occupied by infectious diseases.

Danger of infectious diseases of people and animals (epidemics and epizootics). There are more than 500 natural foci of anthrax in the Republic, there are natural foci of rabies, which can lead to mass diseases of domestic animals and people in Stolin, Ivanovo, Pinsk districts of Brest region; Dubrovensk, Lioznensk, Dokshitsk, Gluboksk districts of Vitebsk region; Volkovysk, Oshmyansk districts of Grodno region; Bykhov, Mstislavl districts of Mogilev region.

Affection of agricultural plants by diseases and pests (epiphytotics). Mass diseases of agricultural crops with brown rust and phytophthora are most widespread in Brest, Grodno and Gomel regions. Occurrence of epizootic and epiphytotic foci on the territory of the Republic can lead to reduction of agricultural production by 5-10%.

3. Recommendations on citizens' actions in case of threat and occurrence of natural emergencies.

Flooding. When warning. To warn about flooding can signal ‘Attention to all!’, broadcast by sirens, intermittent beeps of enterprises and transporters. Having heard the signal, switch on the radio, TV and listen to information and instructions to the population (Fig. 1).

An example of a message about flooding. Attention! Says the Department of Civil Defence and Emergency Situations. Citizens! Due to the rising water level in the river Erokha is expected to flood houses in the area of streets Svadkovskogo, Trifonova. The population living in these streets, collect necessary things, food and water, switch off gas and electricity, go to the area of Mount Morgun for evacuation to a safe zone.

When receiving information about the beginning of evacuation, you should quickly gather and take with you: a bag with documents and money; medical kit; three-day supply of food; bed linen and toiletries; a set of outer clothing and shoes.

All evacuees must arrive by the established deadline at the evacuation point for registration and dispatch to a safe area. Depending on the current situation, the population is evacuated by specially allocated for these purposes transport or on foot.

Figure 1. Actions of the population in case of advance notification of flooding

Upon arrival at the final point, registration is carried out and dispatch to places of accommodation for temporary residence is organised.

In case of flash flooding (Fig. 2) it is recommended to occupy the nearest safe elevated place as quickly as possible and be ready for organised evacuation by water with the help of various watercraft or on foot by fords. In such a situation should not give in to panic, lose composure.

It is necessary to take measures that allow rescuers to timely find people cut off by water and in need of help. During daylight hours it is achieved by hanging a white or coloured cloth on a high place, and at night - by giving light signals.

Figure 2. Action of the population in case of flash flooding

Until help arrives, people in the flood zone should stay on the upper floors and roofs of buildings, trees and other elevated places. Staying in the flood zone usually lasts until the water recedes or help arrives.

When in the water, do not panic. Figure 3 describes what to do in such cases.

Hurricanes, storms, tornadoes. The main signs of hurricanes, storms and tornadoes are: increased wind speed and a sharp drop in atmospheric pressure; heavy rainfall and storm surge of water; heavy snowfall and ground dust.

When alerting. Information about the threat of hurricanes, storms and tornadoes carries information about the onset of natural phenomenon in a particular area, the possible character of its action and the behaviour of people in the situation (Fig. 4).

An example of a message about the threat of a hurricane.

Attention! Says the Department of Civil Defence and Emergency Situations. Citizens! Hurricane is approaching the city. His approach is expected in 2 hours from the south-eastern side of the city. Put out fire in cookers, close doors and windows, attic and ventilation hatches; fix everything that can be carried away by the hurricane; take places in strong buildings, basements, shelters. On the passage of the hurricane you will be informed by radio broadcasting network.

On the windward side of buildings tightly close windows, doors, attic hatches and vents. Glass windows and showcases are taped, protected by shutters or shields. To equalise the internal pressure, doors and windows on the leeward side of buildings are opened.

It is necessary to prepare electric lanterns, candles, camp cookers, supplies of food, drinking water and medicines.

Upon receiving information about the immediate approach of a hurricane or a strong storm, people occupy previously prepared places in buildings or shelters, and in case of a tornado - only basements and underground structures.

Fig 4. Actions of the population during advance warning of the threat of hurricanes, storms and tornadoes

In a sudden onset hurricane (Figure 5). When in a building, beware of injury from shards of window glass. In case of strong gusts of wind it is necessary to move away from windows and take a place in niches of walls, doorways or stand close to the wall, as well as use strong furniture and mattresses.

When forced to stay under the open sky, it is desirable as far as possible away from the buildings and take for shelter gullies, pits, ditches, ditches, ditches, ditches of roads; at the same time it is necessary to lie down in them and tightly pressed to the ground. Such actions significantly reduce the number of injuries caused by throwing action of hurricanes and storms, as well as fully provide protection from flying shards of glass, bricks and other objects. It is not recommended to be on bridges, in places of immediate proximity to objects with chemically dangerous and flammable substances. It is possible to protect from flying fragments and glass shards by sheets of plywood, cardboard and plastic boxes, boards and other improvised means (Fig. 6).

When accompanying hurricanes and storms with thunderstorms, it is necessary to avoid situations in which the probability of electric shocks increases. Therefore, one should not take shelter under freestanding trees, poles and masts, close to the supports of power lines.
